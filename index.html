Hello, my name is Yuhai Wang. I am a current Master of Science in Analytics student at the University of Southern California. I completed my Bachelor of Science in Internet of Things Engineering at Tiangong University in China with a GPA of 3.7/4. During my undergraduate studies, I gained experience in programming languages such as Python, MATLAB, and C, as well as cloud computing, database principles, and applications.

In 2022, I have worked as a research engineer at Institute for AI Industry Research (AIR) of Tsinghua University. In this role, I utilized various programming languages to complete robotics-related tasks, including gait planning of a biped robot, solving the inverse kinematics of robotic arms, and integrating motor and vision systems of a robot arm by using ROS in the Linux environment. I also contributed to the development of the simulator and competition system for RoboMaster University Sim2Real Challenge at IEEE ICRA 2022, resulting in a successful competition.

In 2021, As a team leader at Tianjin Key Laboratory of Autonomous Intelligence Technology and Systems, I designed a novel transformable mechanism based on four-bar linkage and calculated the robot's kinematics and inverse kinematics. I created a simulation robot model using Webots and implemented movement functions via C to simulate two different movements of the robot. I also assembled a robot prototype with 3D printing components and customized parts and developed controlling programs using MATLAB. And also published a paper Lywal: a Leg-Wheel Transformable Quadruped Robot with Picking up and Transport Functions, 2021 IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA), 2021. (Xue Yongjiang, Yuan Xichen, Wang Yuhai et al.)
